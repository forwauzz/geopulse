## Problem
- core problem: many teams still write websites and documentation as if only humans will evaluate them, even though agents are increasingly doing the discovery, comparison, and implementation work
- why it matters now: if agents cannot understand your docs, structure, and product claims quickly, they may route demand toward competitors with clearer machine-readable surfaces

## GEO-Pulse angle
- what GEO-Pulse can credibly say: GEO-Pulse can explain why agent-readable documentation and site structure matter for AI-mediated discovery, using the YC discussion as directional framing and Supabase docs as a practical example of structured, usable documentation
- what GEO-Pulse must not claim: do not imply one video proves a universal market rule; do not claim Supabase wins only because of documentation; do not promise documentation changes alone will cause citations or revenue lifts

## Key points
- "make something agents want" applies to marketing and documentation, not only APIs
- agents choose tools from what they can parse, compare, and act on
- documentation is now part of your acquisition surface
- Supabase docs are a useful example because they are structured, explicit, and operational rather than purely promotional
- a site should still explain itself clearly in text even when video or images are present
