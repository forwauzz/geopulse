# Article Brief

## Asset
- type: article
- target persona: SEO consultants
- secondary persona: in-house content teams
- working title: Crawlable but Not Extractable: The Gap Most Teams Miss

## Problem
- core problem: teams assume that if a page is reachable, it is also usable to AI systems
- why it matters now: many sites are technically accessible but still fail to present clear, quotable, machine-legible answers

## GEO-Pulse angle
- what GEO-Pulse can credibly say: GEO-Pulse can explain the difference between crawl access and extractability through observable page structure, answer placement, segmentation, and trust signals
- what GEO-Pulse must not claim: do not imply GEO-Pulse can predict citation outcomes or prove exact AI-answer representation from one page alone

## Key points
- crawl access is necessary but not enough
- a page can be indexable and still hard to segment or summarize
- extractability depends on answer placement, structure, terminology, and context
- many teams fix technical access first and stop too early
- the right CTA is to run a free scan and use it as a structural baseline
