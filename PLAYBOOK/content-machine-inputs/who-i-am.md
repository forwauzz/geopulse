## What I Do

### 1. CEO of ALIE
At ALIE, my role is not just to “run a startup.” I am actively shaping the product, the strategy, the customer understanding, the positioning, and the long-term vision.

ALIE is a high-stakes medico-legal and clinical documentation platform. My work here involves:
- defining the product vision
- identifying the real pain points for doctors, lawyers, and clinical users
- translating those pain points into workflows and product features
- helping shape the user experience and product architecture
- thinking deeply about compliance, defensibility, structure, and practical usability
- pushing the company toward something truly differentiated, not just another generic AI tool

I care a lot about building ALIE into a serious, durable product that can stand up in real clinical and medico-legal environments.

### 2. Chief Product Officer of Vision (now Aurion)
In Vision / Aurion, I focus on product strategy, user needs, experience design, workflow logic, and product direction.

My role is centered on:
- understanding the end user deeply
- turning abstract ideas into practical product systems
- helping define what should be built and why
- ensuring the product remains useful, focused, and coherent
- keeping the product aligned with real-world value rather than hype

I naturally spend a lot of time in product thinking, UX thinking, feature prioritization, and making sure the product direction stays sharp.

### 3. Clinical Operations Consultant — Teché Health Services
Through Teché Health Services, I work as a clinical operations consultant.

This part of my work is very grounded in the real day-to-day problems of clinics and healthcare practices. I think about:
- workflow inefficiencies
- operational bottlenecks
- staff training
- EMR-related processes
- how technology can improve practice efficiency without disrupting operations
- how to make implementation practical, not theoretical

This consulting work keeps me close to the actual environment in which many healthcare tools succeed or fail.
