## Strong repeated pain points

- many teams are technically crawlable but not extractable
- schema and machine-readable structure are repeatedly mentioned as important
- direct answers, clear definitions, and FAQ-style formatting matter
- weak trust signals and fuzzy positioning reduce citability
- internal linking and topical clustering help AI systems understand context
- brochure-style sites are hard for AI systems to interpret
- some teams accidentally block or degrade AI access through robots, WAF, or JS-heavy pages
- there is frustration that volume and keyword work are no longer enough on their own
- there is skepticism toward tools that generate hype or content without diagnosing visibility gaps

## Strong repeated language

- crawlable but not extractable
- clarity, not volume
- make it easy to cite
- citation-friendly
- clear definitions
- concise summaries
- direct answers
- trust signals
- authority signals
- sites look like brochures

## Messaging rules implied by research

- do not make GEO-Pulse sound like a generic AI content generator
- do not overclaim around rankings or citations
- keep the promise diagnostic and practical
- emphasize useful visibility diagnostics over vanity scoring
- position the product as the tool that helps teams understand what is actually broken
