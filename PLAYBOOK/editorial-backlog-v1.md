# GEO-Pulse Editorial Backlog V1

Last updated: 2026-03-31

## Purpose

Define the first editorial backlog for the GEO-Pulse content machine.

This backlog is designed to:
- match the current persona priority order
- support the `free scan` CTA
- reinforce GEO-Pulse positioning around readiness, not rankings
- produce content that is useful on-site and usable in a newsletter-first distribution model

This is a planning artifact only.

## Current audience priority

1. SEO consultants
2. agency owners
3. ecommerce operators
4. founders
5. growth marketers
6. in-house content teams

## Selection rules

The first backlog should favor topics that:
- map directly to repeated market pain points
- naturally support a free-scan CTA
- let GEO-Pulse speak from product truth
- are specific enough to teach, not just comment
- can become both an article and a newsletter issue

The first backlog should avoid:
- generic AI-search trend commentary
- speculative GEO futurism
- vendor-comparison content too early
- benchmark-style claims the product cannot support yet

## Priority labels

- `P1`: write early
- `P2`: write after first 3 to 5 are shipped
- `P3`: useful, but should wait until the core content system is working

## Suggested first writing order

The first 5 pieces should likely be:

1. How to Audit Your Site for AI Search Readiness
2. Crawlable but Not Extractable: The Gap Most Teams Miss
3. Why Schema Is Necessary but Not Sufficient
4. What Makes a Site Easy to Cite
5. Why Some Sites Read Like Brochures Instead of Answers

Reason:
- they match the highest-ranked audiences
- they align tightly to repeated language from the research
- they support the free-scan CTA naturally
- they define GEO-Pulse's point of view early

## Newsletter-first packaging

For the first 5 topics:
- publish the canonical article on GEO-Pulse
- derive one newsletter issue from the same brief
- keep the newsletter sharper and shorter than the article
- point readers back to the canonical article or free-scan entry point

## Do-not-write-yet list

Hold these until the first backlog is working:
- broad "future of GEO" manifestos
- "top GEO tools" comparisons
- hard-stat benchmark pieces without verification
- competitor callout content
- highly tactical channel-specific content for platforms you are not integrating yet

## Review checkpoint

Before moving from planning to implementation, review:
- whether this backlog is too SEO-heavy
- whether ecommerce needs to move higher in the first 5
- whether one founder-facing piece should move into the first 3 for broader top-of-funnel reach
